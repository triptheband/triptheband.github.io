About

Shaun Rammers – tenor sax | Ellie Lamb – trombone | Ronny Ferella – drums and clarinet

1994 – the year 20 minute quarters were introduced to the AFL, Blue Heelers premiered on channel 7 and most importantly of all Joey Barron Released his album Raised Pleasure Dot, changing the classic lineup of saxophone, trombone and drums forever. The music on this album was high energy and pushed the limits of what three humans could do together. Featuring such hits as Unleashing the Dobermans and Hey Hockaloogie, Shaun Rammers (sax), Ellie Lamb (trombone) and Ronny Ferella (drums) have joined forces to celebrate this amazing album, along with original music of their own. Time to party like it’s 1994!
